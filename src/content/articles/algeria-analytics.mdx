On the FIFA ranking Algeria sit 29, and the Elo model has them at 1785, which frames them as a seeded side with real quality. Vladimir Petkovic's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 1 and losing 2 of 4 games while scoring 5 and conceding 9. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

They sit in the middle of the spectrum, neither dominant nor fragile, a side that has to earn everything it gets. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.14 expected goals for and 1.52 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they lost 0-3 against Argentina, and the expected goals (0.3 to 1.5) had them second best on the run of play. No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In their second group game they won 2-1 against Jordan, and they carried the greater threat, 1.9 to 0.6 on xG. Control when it mattered, and enough at the other end to see it through. In their third group game they drew 3-3 against Austria, and it stayed tight on xG, 1.6 to 1.4. An even game that an even result did justice to. In the round-of-32 tie they lost 0-2 to Switzerland, and the run of chances favoured Switzerland, 0.7 to 2.6 on xG. The better side went through, and the underlying numbers do not argue otherwise.

## Attack

In attack the output matched the process, 5 goals from around 4.5 expected, which is the healthiest place to be: winning on repeatable chance-creation rather than hot streaks. Their most threatening display came against Jordan, worth 1.9 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Riyad Mahrez (38), Mohamed Amoura (19), Amine Gouiri (10), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 9 from around 6.1 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Switzerland, who worked 2.6 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 4 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Riyad Mahrez (2) heading a list that also features Rafik Belghali (1), Nadhir Benbouali (1), Amine Gouiri (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

## The key men

Individually, Riyad Mahrez has been the driver, 2 goals and 1 assist in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.73. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

The team news matters too. Availability doubts hang over M. Amoura (hamstring injury), and how many of those clear up before kickoff shapes what this side can actually put on the pitch.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms. A goal difference of -4 from 3 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 23rd for expected goals created and 19th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 23rd for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

## The reckoning

Their tournament is over. It ended in the round of 32, a 0-2 defeat to Switzerland on 3 July. The margins told the story, and the better side went through. The profile above is the record they leave behind, and the underlying numbers say they were closer to the level than the exit alone suggests.
