On the FIFA ranking Algeria sit 29, and the Elo model has them at 1785, which frames them as a seeded side with real quality. Vladimir Petkovic's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 1 and losing 1 of 3 games while scoring 5 and conceding 7. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

They sit in the middle of the spectrum, neither dominant nor fragile, a side that has to earn everything it gets. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.27 expected goals for and 1.18 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they lost 0-3 against Argentina, and the expected goals (0.3 to 1.5) had them second best on the run of play. No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In their second group game they won 2-1 against Jordan, and they carried the greater threat, 1.9 to 0.6 on xG. Control when it mattered, and enough at the other end to see it through. In their third group game they drew 3-3 against Austria, and it stayed tight on xG, 1.6 to 1.4. An even game that an even result did justice to.

In attack the output matched the process, 5 goals from around 3.8 expected, which is the healthiest place to be: winning on repeatable chance-creation rather than hot streaks. Their most threatening display came against Jordan, worth 1.9 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Riyad Mahrez (38), Mohamed Amoura (19), Amine Gouiri (10), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 7 from around 3.5 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Argentina, who worked 1.5 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 3 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Riyad Mahrez (2) heading a list that also features Rafik Belghali (1), Nadhir Benbouali (1), Amine Gouiri (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

Individually, Riyad Mahrez has been the driver, 2 goals and 1 assist in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.73. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

The team news matters too. Availability doubts hang over M. Amoura (hamstring injury), and how many of those clear up before kickoff shapes what this side can actually put on the pitch.

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms.
