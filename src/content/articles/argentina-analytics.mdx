On the FIFA ranking Argentina sit 2, and the Elo model has them at 2148, which frames them as one of the tournament favourites. Lionel Scaloni's side topped their group, winning 3, drawing 0 and losing 0 of 3 games while scoring 8 and conceding 1. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

This is a front-foot side that both makes chances and denies them, the profile of a team built to go deep. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.99 expected goals for and 0.52 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they won 3-0 against Algeria, and the expected-goals split (1.5 to 0.3) says they made the better chances. It was the kind of afternoon where everything they tried came off. In their second group game they won 2-0 against Austria, and they carried the greater threat, 2.4 to 0.5 on xG. Control when it mattered, and enough at the other end to see it through. In their third group game they won 3-1 against Jordan, and the openings fell their way, 2.1 expected goals to 0.7. Not spectacular, but exactly the sort of result good sides grind out.

## Attack

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 8 goals from roughly 6.0 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Austria, worth 2.4 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Lionel Messi (116), Lautaro Martinez (36), Julian Alvarez (14), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 1 and allowing only about 1.6 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Jordan, who worked 0.7 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 2 clean sheets from 3 games.

In this tournament the scoring has leaned on Lionel Messi, whose 6 make up over half of the group-stage goals. That is production and dependency in one number: mark him out and the supply can dry up.

## The key men

Individually, Lionel Messi has been the driver, 6 goals in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 9.27. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of +7 from 6 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 11th for expected goals created and 7th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 3rd for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

Our model, built off that 2148 Elo rating, makes them about 97% to come through, and the numbers expect them to win, though one knockout tie is a blunt instrument and favourites get caught every round. The profile above is the case for and against them; the next ninety minutes are where it gets tested.
