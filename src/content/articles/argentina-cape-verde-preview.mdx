A country of half a million people, at their first World Cup, have been handed the champions. On paper it is the definition of a free hit.

Argentina are exactly what you remember. Lionel Messi, 38 and still the best passer in the tournament, with Lautaro Martínez and Julián Álvarez to finish what he starts. Lionel Scaloni's side don't need to be spectacular to win; they need about twenty good minutes, and they usually get them.

Cape Verde have made a habit of not reading the script. Ryan Mendes has 22 goals for the Blue Sharks and the kind of tournament calm you can't coach, and behind him a back line that has frustrated far bigger names to get here. They will sit deep, stay compact, and dare Argentina to be patient.

The model gives Cape Verde a 2% chance and the market a slightly kinder 5%, which is the market's way of saying "stranger things." Argentina should win, and win well. But every so often the underdog gets one shot and takes it, and this is the World Cup, so you watch.
