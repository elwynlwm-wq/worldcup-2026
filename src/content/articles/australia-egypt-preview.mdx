This is one of the more even ties of the round, two well-drilled, hard-working sides separated by a handful of rating points and, potentially, by one extraordinary player.

Egypt have the star quality. Mohamed Salah, with 67 international goals, is comfortably the most decisive attacker on the pitch, and around him Hossam Hassan's side have looked solid: a 3-1 win over New Zealand and creditable 1-1 draws with both Iran and Belgium, the latter a genuine statement of resilience against a fancied opponent. The worry is availability elsewhere — Hamdi Fathy is carrying a muscle bruise and Hossam Abdelmaguid is suspended, thinning the squad around their talisman.

## By the numbers

Australia are exactly the sort of opponent who make life difficult. Tony Popovic's side are organised, physical and dangerous from set pieces, with Jackson Irvine and Mathew Leckie the driving forces and Harry Souttar a threat at both ends. They beat Türkiye 2-0 and ground out a goalless draw with Paraguay, and while a 2-0 loss to the USA showed their ceiling, this is a team that rarely beats itself. Jacob Italiano's adductor injury is a minor selection wrinkle.

History leans Egypt's way — the sides have met twice, with Egypt winning the last, a 3-0 result in 2010 — but that is ancient enough to mean little here. The likelier shape is a cagey, tight contest in which Australia try to stifle Salah, keep it low-scoring, and pinch something from a set piece, while Egypt look to get their number ten on the ball in dangerous areas.

We give Egypt a slight edge, almost entirely on the basis that Salah is the best route to a goal on either side, and knockout ties this tight often turn on a single piece of quality. But this is close to a coin-flip, and an Australia side this disciplined is more than capable of grinding it out.

## The call

Prediction: Egypt to edge it, with Salah the difference, but do not be surprised if Australia drag it to extra time.
