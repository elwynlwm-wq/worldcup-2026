On the FIFA ranking Austria sit 22, and the Elo model has them at 1836, which frames them as a seeded side with real quality. Ralf Rangnick's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 1 and losing 1 of 3 games while scoring 6 and conceding 6. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

They sit in the middle of the spectrum, neither dominant nor fragile, a side that has to earn everything it gets. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.22 expected goals for and 1.71 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they won 3-1 against Jordan, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.7 to 1.1 on xG). A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for. In their second group game they lost 0-2 against Argentina, and they were out-created, 0.5 to 2.4 on xG. No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In their third group game they drew 3-3 against Algeria, and it stayed tight on xG, 1.4 to 1.6. An even game that an even result did justice to.

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 6 goals from roughly 3.7 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Jordan, worth 1.7 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Marko Arnautovic (47), Marcel Sabitzer (26), Michael Gregoritsch (24), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

Defensively they were stretched, shipping 6 and giving up close to 5.1 expected across the tracked games. Against better forwards that leak becomes the story, and it is the clearest thing standing between them and a longer run. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Argentina, who worked 2.4 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 3 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Marko Arnautovic (2) heading a list that also features Yazan Al-Arab (1), Sasa Kalajdzic (1), Romano Schmid (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

Individually, Marko Arnautović has been the driver, 2 goals in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 6.97. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms. A goal difference of +0 from 3 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 25th for expected goals created and 15th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 36th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.
