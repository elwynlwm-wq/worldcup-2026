Belgium came into the 2026 World Cup as a genuine contender, seeded 10 in the world and rated 1884 on Elo. Rudi Garcia's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 2 and losing 0 of 3 games while scoring 6 and conceding 2. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

This is a front-foot side that both makes chances and denies them, the profile of a team built to go deep. Across the games tracked they averaged 2.25 expected goals for and 0.65 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they draw 1-1 against Egypt, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.4 to 1.1 on xG). An even game that an even result did justice to. In their second group game they draw 0-0 against Iran, and the expected-goals split (1.8 to 0.6) says they made the better chances. Two points dropped more than one earned, on the balance of the chances. In their third group game they win 5-1 against New Zealand, and the expected-goals split (3.6 to 0.2) says they made the better chances. The margin flattered no one; they were that far ahead.

In attack the output matched the process, 6 goals from around 6.7 expected, which is the healthiest place to be: winning on repeatable chance-creation rather than hot streaks. Their most threatening display came against New Zealand, worth 3.6 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Romelu Lukaku (90), Kevin De Bruyne (31), Youri Tielemans (13), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 2 and allowing only about 1.9 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Egypt, who worked 1.1 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 1 clean sheet from 3 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Leandro Trossard (2) heading a list that also features Romelu Lukaku (1), Mohamed Hany (1), Kevin De Bruyne (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of +4 from 2 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

They are into the knockouts now, where the 1884 rating and the xG edge matter less than holding a lead for ninety tense minutes. On this evidence they have the tools; whether they have the nerve is the one thing no model prices.
