Belgium came into the 2026 World Cup as a genuine contender, seeded 10 in the world and rated 1884 on Elo. Rudi Garcia's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 3, drawing 2 and losing 0 of 5 games while scoring 13 and conceding 5. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

This is a front-foot side that both makes chances and denies them, the profile of a team built to go deep. Across the games tracked they averaged 2.21 expected goals for and 0.65 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they drew 1-1 against Egypt, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.4 to 1.1 on xG). An even game that an even result did justice to. In their second group game they drew 0-0 against Iran, and they carried the greater threat, 1.8 to 0.6 on xG. Two points dropped more than one earned, on the balance of the chances. In their third group game they won 5-1 against New Zealand, and the openings fell their way, 3.6 expected goals to 0.2. The margin flattered no one; they were that far ahead. In the round-of-32 tie they beat Senegal 3-2 after extra time. A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for. ## Attack

In attack the output matched the process, 13 goals from around 8.8 expected, which is the healthiest place to be: winning on repeatable chance-creation rather than hot streaks. Their most threatening display came against New Zealand, worth 3.6 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Romelu Lukaku (90), Kevin De Bruyne (31), Youri Tielemans (13), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 5 and allowing only about 2.6 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Egypt, who worked 1.1 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 1 clean sheet from 5 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Leandro Trossard (2) heading a list that also features Romelu Lukaku (1), Mohamed Hany (1), Kevin De Bruyne (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

## The key men

Individually, Leandro Trossard has been the driver, 2 goals in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 8.03. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

The team news matters too. Availability doubts hang over Z. Debast (injury), J. Doku (illness), N. Ngoy (suspended), and how many of those clear up before kickoff shapes what this side can actually put on the pitch.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of +8 from 2 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 3rd for expected goals created and 15th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 7th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.
