On paper this is a meeting of star names. In practice it is a meeting of two sides who have looked some way short of their best, which makes it one of the more genuinely open ties of the round.

Belgium have the bigger reputations, Kevin De Bruyne still pulling the strings, Romelu Lukaku and his 90 international goals up top, and they signed off the group with a thumping 5-0 win over New Zealand. But the underlying form was patchy, a 1-1 draw with Egypt and a goalless one with Iran, and Rudi Garcia's squad is carrying knocks: Jérémy Doku is battling illness, Zeno Debast is injured, and Nathan Ngoy is suspended after a red card. A Belgium missing its best dribbler and a first-choice defender is a beatable Belgium.

Senegal will fancy that. Pape Thiaw's side recovered from group defeats to France and Norway with an emphatic 5-0 win over Iraq that got them through, and they carry serious firepower of their own in Sadio Mané, whose 55 international goals match Lukaku's threat, and the pacey Ismaila Sarr. The concern is at the other end: goalkeeper Édouard Mendy is nursing a twisted knee, and if he cannot go, it removes one of the calmest heads in the side against exactly the kind of attack that punishes hesitation.

So the tie hinges on which set of absences bites harder. Belgium's midfield quality, with De Bruyne dictating, should give them control, but Senegal's athleticism and directness are tailor-made to exploit a defence shorn of Debast and a team not quite firing. If it becomes a track meet, Senegal are more than capable; if Belgium slow it down and let De Bruyne conduct, their ceiling is higher.

Our lean is Belgium, on the strength of their midfield and Lukaku's finishing, but with real hesitation. This has the makings of a tight, physical contest that could go either way and would not be a surprise if it needed extra time.

Prediction: Belgium to edge a close one, but Senegal have the weapons to make it a long night, and an upset here would shock nobody.
