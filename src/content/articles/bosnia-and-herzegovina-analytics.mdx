On the FIFA ranking Bosnia and Herzegovina sit 61, and the Elo model has them at 1622, which frames them as one of the field's longshots. Sergej Barbarez's side scraped through among the best third-placed sides, winning 1, drawing 1 and losing 2 of 4 games while scoring 5 and conceding 8. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

They sit in the middle of the spectrum, neither dominant nor fragile, a side that has to earn everything it gets. Across the games tracked they averaged 0.52 expected goals for and 1.25 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they drew 1-1 against Canada, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.0 to 1.2 on xG). An even game that an even result did justice to. In their second group game they lost 1-4 against Switzerland, and they were out-created, 0.2 to 2.1 on xG. No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In their third group game they won 3-1 against Qatar, and it stayed tight on xG, 0.7 to 0.8. The scoreline was kinder than the performance, and they will know it. In the round-of-32 tie they lost 0-2 to United States, and the run of chances favoured United States, 0.2 to 0.9 on xG. The better side went through, and the underlying numbers do not argue otherwise.

## Attack

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 5 goals from roughly 2.1 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Canada, worth 1.0 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Edin Džeko (73), Ermedin Demirović (4), Haris Tabaković (4), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 8 from around 5.0 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Switzerland, who worked 2.1 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 4 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Ermin Mahmic (2) heading a list that also features Mahmoud Abunada (1), Kerim Alajbegovic (1), Jovo Lukić (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

## The key men

Individually, Ermin Mahmić has been the driver, 2 goals in 2 appearances at an average match rating of 7.55. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

The team news matters too. Availability doubts hang over T. Muharemović (suspended), and how many of those clear up before kickoff shapes what this side can actually put on the pitch.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers carry a warning: they were out-chanced more often than the table shows, and results built on that footing tend not to hold up against the best. A goal difference of -3 from 4 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 40th for expected goals created and 19th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 27th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

## The reckoning

Their tournament is over. It ended in the round of 32, a 0-2 defeat to United States on 2 July. The margins told the story, and the better side went through. The profile above is the record they leave behind, and the underlying numbers say they were closer to the level than the exit alone suggests.
