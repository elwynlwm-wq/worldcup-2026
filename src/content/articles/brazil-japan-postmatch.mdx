Our model made Brazil favourites at around 55%, and the market agreed. Both were right about the result and wrong about the manner of it, because for long stretches this was Japan's game.

Hajime Moriyasu's side did exactly what we warned they would: they pressed in coordinated waves, rotated cleverly, and made a heavyweight uncomfortable. Kaishū Sano's 29th-minute opener was no smash-and-grab. It was the logical reward for a side that had been the braver, sharper team, moving Brazil around and picking the moments to strike. At the interval, the tournament's most functional favourites looked rattled.

Carlo Ancelotti's response was the story of Brazil's tournament so far: not brilliance, but weight. Casemiro's equaliser just after the hour was scruffy and deserved in equal measure, the product of Brazil finally turning territory into pressure. From there it became the game Japan feared, waves of Brazilian substitutes against tiring legs, and the longer it stayed level the more it favoured the side with the deeper bench.

Still, it needed the very last kick. Gabriel Martinelli, on to change it, arrived in the 96th minute to break Japanese hearts, and a scoreline that reads 2-1 will, in a week, look far more comfortable than the ninety minutes that produced it.

What does it tell us? Two things. Brazil are winning without convincing, and against France or Spain that margin disappears. And Japan, again, proved they are nobody's easy afternoon. The gap between the best-drilled side in Asia and a Selecao stacked with talent was a single moment of quality and a bench Japan could not match. That is not a gulf. It is a detail.

Brazil march into the last 16, still the model's pick to go deep, still not the finished article. The talent has never been in doubt. The question Ancelotti still has to answer is whether ruthless late wins are a sign of champion's resolve or a warning that the performances have to catch up with the results. Japan go home with their heads high, and with the quiet satisfaction of having been, for an hour, the better team at a World Cup.
