There is the story you would tell from the scoreboard, and the story the data tells, and this time they pull in opposite directions. Japan led at half-time and took Brazil to the 96th minute, which sounds like a side that came within a whisker of a famous win. The expected goals say something else entirely: Brazil 1.69, Japan 0.23. This was not a smash-and-grab that got away. It was a Brazil siege that took an age to land.

Kaishū Sano's opener on 29 minutes was the definition of against the run of play, a rare Japanese foray finished with real quality while Brazil were already dominating territory and chances. From there Carlo Ancelotti's side did everything but score, and the frustration was as much about their own wastefulness as any Japanese resistance. Casemiro's equaliser just after the hour was overdue on the balance of play, and by the time Gabriel Martinelli arrived in stoppage time to win it, Brazil had racked up the kind of xG advantage that usually settles a game by the hour mark.

So the honest read is more flattering to Brazil than the 2-1 suggests, and less flattering than it looks too. They created plenty, which is encouraging; they needed 96 minutes to beat a Japan side that mustered 0.23 expected goals, which is not. A cleaner team puts this to bed early. Ancelotti will take the win and the clean-sheet-that-wasn't, and quietly note that the finishing has to sharpen before France or Spain.

Japan, for their part, leave with pride but few illusions. Sano's goal and a disciplined shape kept them alive, but they barely threatened a second, and over 90 minutes they were comfortably second best in everything except the half-hour they led. Hajime Moriyasu's side remain a well-drilled, likeable team that lacked the cutting edge to punish a wasteful favourite.

Brazil march on, unconvincing in front of goal but dominant in the underlying numbers, which is a strange and slightly ominous combination. The performances still trail the results. But on this evidence, the chances are coming in a flood. Sooner or later, against a lesser goalkeeper or a tiring defence, Brazil are going to bury one of these games early and remind everyone what they can be.
