If you are hunting a shock in the last 16, start here, because the numbers and the history point in opposite directions.

Our model and the market both make Brazil favourites, and on raw talent that is correct: Carlo Ancelotti's side beat Japan 2-1 to get here and carry the deeper squad. But two things complicate the story. The first is the injury list. Brazil arrive without Neymar, nursing a calf problem, and Raphinha, out with a hamstring injury, which strips Ancelotti of two of his most creative forwards in a single blow. A Brazil attack leaning on Lucas Paquetá and a not-fully-firing supporting cast is a very different proposition to the one the seeding assumes.

## By the numbers

The second is history, and it is remarkable. In four meetings, Brazil have never beaten Norway, who hold two wins and two draws in the series. It is one of international football's odd little curses, and it exists because Norway have always been exactly the kind of physical, direct, fearless opponent that unsettles the Selecao. This is not a coincidence Brazil will enjoy being reminded of.

And this Norway are dangerous in a very modern way. They edged Ivory Coast 2-1 in the last round, and their whole method reduces to one brutal fact: they have Erling Haaland, 55 goals in 50 internationals, the most reliable finisher on earth. Ståle Solbakken's team do not always control matches, but they do not need to. Keep it tight, feed the big man one chance, and tight games become results. With Alexander Sørloth and Antonio Nusa around him, the supply is real.

So the shape of the tie is clear. Brazil will have the ball and the territory; the question is whether a depleted attack can turn that into goals against a side happy to sit, absorb and counter. If Brazil score early, their quality tells and the record is broken. If it stays level into the last half hour, every ghost of those four unbeaten meetings starts to whisper, and Haaland only needs one.

Our call leans Brazil, because class and depth usually win out, but with far less conviction than a match between the fifth and twenty-third ranked sides should carry. The absences of Neymar and Raphinha matter, the history matters, and Haaland is the great equaliser.

## The call

Prediction: Brazil to edge it, but this is the tie most likely to spring a surprise, and we would not blink if Norway extended a record Brazil would love to end.
