The model is unambiguous: Brazil are heavy favourites, and Scotland, for all their honest qualities, are simply outmatched. The intrigue here is not the result but the manner, how much of themselves Carlo Ancelotti's side choose to show.

Brazil have the talent to win this at a stroll. Vinícius Júnior and Raphinha either side of the attack, Bruno Guimarães controlling midfield, and enough individual quality that one moment can settle it early. Under Ancelotti they have leaned towards efficiency over exhibition, banking results while conserving energy, and a group game against Scotland is exactly the sort of fixture where that pragmatism makes sense.

Scotland will compete, they always do, pressing honestly and making themselves awkward. But competing and threatening are different things, and it is hard to see Steve Clarke's side creating enough to trouble Alisson over 90 minutes. Their best hope is to stay in the game long enough to frustrate, and pray Brazil's minds have already wandered to the knockouts.

For Brazil, the professional move is clear: score, control, and get out with a clean sheet and fresh legs. Whether they choose to dazzle or simply do the job is the only real question.

Prediction: a comfortable Brazil win. Expect them to take the lead, manage the game, and keep plenty in reserve for the rounds that matter.
