The Round of 16, a full house behind the hosts, and the one team in the draw that treats defending like an art form. Canada's summer has been a story; Morocco are here to end it.

Canada have ridden the noise. Alphonso Davies is a matchup problem for anyone down the left, Jonathan David gives them a real number nine, and a home crowd has dragged them through moments they might otherwise have lost. Belief is a genuine weapon and they have it.

Morocco don't especially care about belief. This is a side built on the run to a World Cup semi-final, with Achraf Hakimi bombing forward from full-back, Sofyan Amrabat shielding everything in front of the back four, and enough quality up top to punish one lapse. They concede almost nothing, and against a young side they will happily make it slow and frustrating.

## By the numbers

The team news hurts Canada most. Ismaël Koné is out with a fractured lower leg and Ali Ahmed Jones is carrying a knock, denting a side that has otherwise been excellent, nine scored and three conceded at a 2.2 expected-goals average. Morocco, group runners-up and yet to lose, have their own edge in Ayoub El Kaabi and the balance to match Canada blow for blow. History leans firmly one way: four meetings, three Moroccan wins and a draw, no Canadian victory, a 2-1 in 2022 the latest. Canada are the better story of this tournament; Morocco are the harder out.

## The call

Both the model and the market lean Morocco, the model at 50% to Canada's 30, the market shorter still. The hosts' ride has been brilliant, and home advantage is real. But this feels like the round it runs into a wall.
