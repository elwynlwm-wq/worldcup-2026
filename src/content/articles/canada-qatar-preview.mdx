Not every group game is a puzzle. The model makes Canada heavy favourites here, and with good reason: they are the better side by a distance, they have home advantage, and Qatar have looked short of both quality and confidence.

For Canada, this is opportunity as much as obligation. Jesse Marsch's team carry genuine attacking threat, Jonathan David through the middle, Alphonso Davies stretching the game down the left, and against a Qatar defence that has struggled for organisation, the chances should come. A nation still chasing its first ever World Cup win could hardly ask for a better stage on which to get it.

Qatar's task is damage limitation, and honestly it is hard to see where their joy comes from. They have offered little going forward and looked vulnerable at the back, and against a co-host roared on by its own crowd, the danger is that a bad day becomes a chastening one.

The only question for Canada is professionalism: take the chances, avoid complacency, and treat the game as a platform to build momentum rather than a box to tick. Do that, and this is the kind of afternoon that can set the tone for a whole tournament.

Prediction: a comfortable Canada win, and potentially a big one. This is the sort of fixture where a side hunting belief can really cut loose.
