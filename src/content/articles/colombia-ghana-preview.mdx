Colombia are favourites here, and both the model and the eye test back it. Néstor Lorenzo's side won their group without fuss, a goalless draw with Portugal, a 1-0 win over DR Congo and a 3-1 defeat of Uzbekistan, and they carry the kind of top-end quality Ghana will struggle to live with.

The names tell the story. James Rodríguez, still Colombia's creative heartbeat with 29 international goals, and Luis Díaz, a genuine game-breaker with 21, give Lorenzo two players capable of settling a knockout tie on their own. Around them Colombia are balanced and experienced, comfortable controlling a game and patient enough to wait for the moment their quality creates.

## By the numbers

Ghana are not here by accident, though, and Carlos Queiroz has made them awkward. They ground out a 1-0 win over Panama, held England to a goalless draw, and only lost narrowly to Croatia, a record that speaks to organisation and resilience rather than flair. In Jordan Ayew, their captain and top scorer with 34 international goals, and the influential Thomas Partey in midfield, they have enough to trouble a side that switches off. Iñaki Williams gives them pace to run in behind.

The tie, then, is a familiar one: class against graft. Ghana's best hope is to make it ugly, keep it tight, frustrate Colombia's rhythm and hope Ayew or Williams can steal a moment. Colombia's task is simply to be patient, trust that James and Díaz will eventually unpick a stubborn block, and avoid the sucker punch on the counter.

We expect Colombia's quality to tell. They are the better side in most areas, and over 90 minutes that usually decides it against a team relying on containment. But Ghana are exactly the sort of opponent who can drag a favourite into a nervy, low-scoring grind.

## The call

Prediction: Colombia to win, likely by a single goal, with Ghana making them earn every yard of it.
