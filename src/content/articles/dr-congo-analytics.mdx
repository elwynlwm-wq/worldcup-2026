DR Congo came into the 2026 World Cup as a dangerous outsider, seeded 46 in the world and rated 1712 on Elo. Sébastien Desabre's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 1 and losing 2 of 4 games while scoring 5 and conceding 5. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

Their identity is control and containment, low-event games decided by the odd moment they are set up to provide. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.09 expected goals for and 0.98 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they drew 1-1 against Portugal, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (0.9 to 0.7 on xG). An even game that an even result did justice to. In their second group game they lost 0-1 against Colombia, and they were out-created, 0.4 to 1.0 on xG. No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In their third group game they won 3-1 against Uzbekistan, and the openings fell their way, 2.4 expected goals to 0.3. Not spectacular, but exactly the sort of result good sides grind out. In the round-of-32 tie they lost 1-2 to England, and the run of chances favoured England, 0.8 to 2.0 on xG. The better side went through, and the underlying numbers do not argue otherwise.

## Attack

In attack the output matched the process, 5 goals from around 4.3 expected, which is the healthiest place to be: winning on repeatable chance-creation rather than hot streaks. Their most threatening display came against Uzbekistan, worth 2.4 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Cédric Bakambu (21), Meschak Elia (12), Yoane Wissa (9), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 5 and allowing only about 3.9 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from England, who worked 2.0 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 4 games.

In this tournament the scoring has leaned on Yoane Wissa, whose 3 make up over half of the group-stage goals. That is production and dependency in one number: mark him out and the supply can dry up.

## The key men

Individually, Yoane Wissa has been the driver, 3 goals in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.27. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.
