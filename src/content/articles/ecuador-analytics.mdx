Ecuador came into the 2026 World Cup as a seeded side with real quality, seeded 24 in the world and rated 1902 on Elo. Sebastián Beccacece's side scraped through among the best third-placed sides, winning 1, drawing 1 and losing 2 of 4 games while scoring 2 and conceding 4. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

Their identity is control and containment, low-event games decided by the odd moment they are set up to provide. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.46 expected goals for and 0.92 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they lost 0-1 against Ivory Coast, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.0 to 1.5 on xG). No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In their second group game they drew 0-0 against Curacao, and they carried the greater threat, 2.8 to 0.5 on xG. Two points dropped more than one earned, on the balance of the chances. In their third group game they won 2-1 against Germany, and the openings fell their way, 1.3 expected goals to 0.7. Not spectacular, but exactly the sort of result good sides grind out. In the round-of-32 tie they lost 0-2 against Mexico, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (0.7 to 1.0 on xG). A defeat that leaves questions without offering many answers.

## Attack

In attack the chances outran the returns: about 5.8 expected goals for only 2 scored. The creation is there, and if the finishing catches up they are more dangerous than the goal tally suggests. Their most threatening display came against Curacao, worth 2.8 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Enner Valencia (49), Gonzalo Plata (8), Félix Torres (5), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 4 from around 3.7 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Ivory Coast, who worked 1.5 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 1 clean sheet from 4 games.

In this tournament the scoring has leaned on Nilson Angulo, whose 1 make up over half of the group-stage goals. That is production and dependency in one number: mark him out and the supply can dry up.

## The key men

Individually, Pedro Vite has been the driver, 1 assist in 4 appearances at an average match rating of 7.47. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of -2 from 4 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 16th for expected goals created and 33rd for goals scored, while the defence ranks 20th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

## What's next

They are into the knockouts, where the 1902 rating and the xG edge matter less than holding a lead for ninety tense minutes.
