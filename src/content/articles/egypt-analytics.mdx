On the FIFA ranking Egypt sit 26, and the Elo model has them at 1742, which frames them as a seeded side with real quality. Hossam Hassan's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 2 and losing 0 of 3 games while scoring 5 and conceding 3. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

They sit in the middle of the spectrum, neither dominant nor fragile, a side that has to earn everything it gets. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.25 expected goals for and 1.47 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they drew 1-1 against Belgium, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.1 to 1.4 on xG). An even game that an even result did justice to. In their second group game they won 3-1 against New Zealand, and they carried the greater threat, 1.9 to 1.2 on xG. Control when it mattered, and enough at the other end to see it through. In their third group game they drew 1-1 against Iran, and the better chances went the other way, 0.8 to 1.8 on expected goals. A point that owed plenty to riding their luck.

## Attack

In attack the output matched the process, 5 goals from around 3.8 expected, which is the healthiest place to be: winning on repeatable chance-creation rather than hot streaks. Their most threatening display came against New Zealand, worth 1.9 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Mohamed Salah (67), Trézéguet (23), Omar Marmoush (11), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 3 from around 4.4 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Iran, who worked 1.8 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 3 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Trézéguet (1) heading a list that also features Mostafa Zico (1), Mohamed Salah (1), Mahmoud Saber (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

## The key men

Individually, Mohamed Salah has been the driver, 1 goal and 2 assists in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.43. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

The team news matters too. Availability doubts hang over Hamdi Fathy (muscle bruise), Hossam Abdelmaguid (suspension through sports court), and how many of those clear up before kickoff shapes what this side can actually put on the pitch.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms. A goal difference of +2 from 4 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.
