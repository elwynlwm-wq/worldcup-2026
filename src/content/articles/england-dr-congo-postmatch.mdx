## How it unfolded

England came into this rated fourth in the world and priced as heavy favourites, and for once the game more or less did what the numbers said it would. Thomas Tuchel's side controlled the tie, built the better chances, and turned that grip into a 2-1 win, with the expected goals reading 2.04 to 0.76 in their favour. That gap is the honest measure of the afternoon: DR Congo were in it on the scoreboard, less so on the play.

Sébastien Desabre's side did what underdogs have to do, staying compact and hanging on to the tie for as long as they could. Their goal kept the closing stages tense and gave the Mercedes-Benz Stadium a nervier finish than England would have wanted. But 0.76 expected goals across ninety minutes is the ceiling of a team that defended for its life rather than one that genuinely threatened an upset.

## What it means

For England this is progress without reassurance. Reaching the last 16 was the minimum expected of a squad this deep, and they got there while doing enough rather than everything. Two goals from two of the better xG performances of the round is a decent return; needing a late steadying against a side ranked 46th is the sort of thing that gets picked over before a harder tie arrives. Tuchel will take the win and the clean progression, and quietly ask for more control late in games.

DR Congo go home with credit and clarity. They rode their luck at times and never really imposed themselves, but they made England work and gave up little for long stretches. On this evidence the gap to the seeded sides is real, though the shape and discipline are a base to build on.

England march on. The performance trailed the billing, but the result did not, and at this stage that is the part that counts.
