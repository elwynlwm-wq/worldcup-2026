England did enough in a competitive group, a 4-2 win over Croatia, a goalless draw with Ghana, and a 2-0 defeat of Panama, and in Harry Kane, still scoring for fun with 78 international goals, plus Bukayo Saka and Marcus Rashford, they have match-winners DR Congo simply cannot match man for man. The one selection headache is at right-back, where Reece James is out with a hamstring injury, forcing a reshuffle in a position England are not especially deep.

DR Congo, though, are no formality. Sébastien Desabre's side punched their ticket the hard way, holding Portugal to a 1-1 draw and beating Uzbekistan 3-1, and they carry a genuine cutting edge through Cédric Bakambu and the Newcastle forward Yoane Wissa, with the vastly experienced Chancel Mbemba marshalling the back line. This is a team that defends with organisation and springs quickly, exactly the profile that can frustrate a favourite into a nervy afternoon.

The two nations have never met at senior level, which adds a note of the unknown to a tie England are expected to control. Their route through is the obvious one: use the ball, stretch DR Congo with Saka and Rashford, and let Kane do the rest. The danger is complacency, a slow start, and a DR Congo side that grows in belief every minute it stays level.

We expect England's quality to tell over 90 minutes. They have too many ways to score and, even reshuffled, enough at the back to handle DR Congo's bursts. But if the Africans nick an early goal and force England to chase, this could get uncomfortable in a hurry.

## The call

Prediction: England to win, probably by a goal or two, but DR Congo have the forwards to make them work for it.
