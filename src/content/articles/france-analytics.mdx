France came into the 2026 World Cup as one of the tournament favourites, seeded 1 in the world and rated 2123 on Elo. Didier Deschamps's side topped their group, winning 4, drawing 0 and losing 0 of 4 games while scoring 13 and conceding 2. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

This is a front-foot side that both makes chances and denies them, the profile of a team built to go deep. Across the games tracked they averaged 2.23 expected goals for and 0.88 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they win 3-1 against Senegal, and the expected-goals split (1.8 to 0.5) says they made the better chances. A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for. In their second group game they win 3-0 against Iraq, and the expected-goals split (2.7 to 0.6) says they made the better chances. A statement result that left little room for argument. In their third group game they win 4-1 against Norway, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.3 to 1.7 on xG). The margin flattered no one; they were that far ahead. In the round-of-32 tie they win 3-0 against Sweden, and the expected-goals split (3.2 to 0.7) says they made the better chances. It was the kind of afternoon where everything they tried came off.

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 13 goals from roughly 8.9 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Sweden, worth 3.2 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Kylian Mbappe (60), Ousmane Dembele (8), Marcus Thuram (7), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 2 and allowing only about 3.5 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Norway, who worked 1.7 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 2 clean sheets from 4 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Ousmane Dembélé (4) heading a list that also features Kylian Mbappé (4), Désiré Doué (1), Bradley Barcola (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of +11 from 6 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

They are into the knockouts now, where the 2123 rating and the xG edge matter less than holding a lead for ninety tense minutes. On this evidence they have the tools; whether they have the nerve is the one thing no model prices.
