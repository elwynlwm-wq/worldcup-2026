France came into the 2026 World Cup as one of the tournament favourites, seeded 1 in the world and rated 2123 on Elo. Didier Deschamps's side topped their group, winning 5, drawing 0 and losing 0 of 5 games while scoring 14 and conceding 2. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

This is a front-foot side that both makes chances and denies them, the profile of a team built to go deep. Across the games tracked they averaged 2.06 expected goals for and 0.73 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## Attack

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 14 goals from roughly 10.3 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Sweden, worth 3.2 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Kylian Mbappe (60), Ousmane Dembele (8), Marcus Thuram (7), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 2 and allowing only about 3.6 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Norway, who worked 1.7 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 3 clean sheets from 5 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Ousmane Dembélé (4) heading a list that also features Kylian Mbappé (4), Désiré Doué (1), Bradley Barcola (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

## The key men

Individually, Kylian Mbappé has been the driver, 6 goals and 2 assists in 4 appearances at an average match rating of 8.65. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of +12 from 6 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 5th for expected goals created and 1st for goals scored, while the defence ranks 17th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.
