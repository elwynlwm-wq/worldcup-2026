Some ties invite debate. This is not one of them. France are the highest-rated team left in the tournament, and our model makes them heavy favourites, comfortably north of 80%, against a Sweden side that is organised, honest, and simply outgunned.

The gap is one of individual quality, and it is vast. Kylian Mbappé is the most decisive attacker in the competition, and around him Didier Deschamps has a squad with match-winners in every line, Ousmane Dembélé, Aurélien Tchouaméni pulling the strings, a defence that barely creaks. France do not need to be brilliant to win this; they need about twenty minutes of their best, and they usually get them.

Sweden's task is containment. Their best hope is to stay compact, frustrate France into impatience, and nick something on a set piece or a transition, the classic underdog blueprint. The trouble is that France are very good at exactly the game Sweden would want to avoid: patient possession, then a sudden burst of quality that settles things before the underdog's plan has had time to work.

If there is a caution for France, it is the oldest one in tournament football, that a comfortable ride can leave a side under-tested when the real examinations come. But that is a worry for later rounds, not for this one.

Prediction: France, and probably at a canter. Expect them to score first, manage the game, and look ominously like the team everyone else is trying to avoid.
