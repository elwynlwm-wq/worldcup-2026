On the FIFA ranking Ghana sit 65, and the Elo model has them at 1575, which frames them as one of the field's longshots. Carlos Queiroz's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 1 and losing 1 of 3 games while scoring 2 and conceding 2. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

Their identity is control and containment, low-event games decided by the odd moment they are set up to provide. Across the games tracked they averaged 0.74 expected goals for and 0.85 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they won 1-0 against Panama, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.3 to 0.7 on xG). A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for. In their second group game they drew 0-0 against England, and they were out-created, 0.2 to 1.4 on xG. A point that owed plenty to riding their luck. In their third group game they lost 1-2 against Croatia, and it stayed tight on xG, 0.7 to 0.5. They deserved more than nothing, which will sting all the more for it.

## Attack

In attack the output matched the process, 2 goals from around 2.2 expected, which is the healthiest place to be: winning on repeatable chance-creation rather than hot streaks. Their most threatening display came against Panama, worth 1.3 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Jordan Ayew (34), Thomas Partey (15), Ernest Nuamah (4), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 2 and allowing only about 2.5 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from England, who worked 1.4 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 2 clean sheets from 3 games.

In this tournament the scoring has leaned on Derrick Luckassen, whose 1 make up over half of the group-stage goals. That is production and dependency in one number: mark him out and the supply can dry up.

## The key men

Individually, Derrick Luckassen has been the driver, 1 goal in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.2. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms. A goal difference of +0 from 4 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 36th for expected goals created and 33rd for goals scored, while the defence ranks 16th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.
