This is the most evenly matched tie of the round, and our model knows it: Norway are favourites, but only just, and the margin is essentially the difference one player can make.

Ivory Coast are a serious side, athletic, quick in transition, and dangerous through Amad Diallo and a front line that carries a real threat. On their day they can beat anyone left in the draw, and they will fancy their pace against a Norway defence that is functional rather than fearsome. If this becomes an open, end-to-end game, the Ivorians have the weapons to win it.

Norway's edge is singular and obvious. Erling Haaland is the most reliable finisher in world football, and a team built around one truly elite striker is a team that turns tight games into results. Norway do not always control matches, they can ride their luck and lean heavily on moments, but when the moment comes, they have the man to take it. That is why the model tilts their way.

The tactical battle, then, is about which side imposes its game. Ivory Coast want it open and chaotic; Norway want it tight enough that a single Haaland chance decides it. Expect a genuine contest, quite possibly level deep into the second half, with the tie hanging on whether Ivory Coast can keep the Norwegian quiet.

## By the numbers

The two nations have never met, which strips the tie back to essentials: Erling Haaland and Alexander Sørloth against an Ivory Coast side that matched its expected goals for and against almost exactly through the group. Norway's ceiling is higher; Ivory Coast's discipline is what keeps them in it.

## The call

Prediction: Norway, narrowly, because the surest way to lose a coin-flip is to leave Haaland even half a yard. But this is the tie most likely to spring a surprise.
