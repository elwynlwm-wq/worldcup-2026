Rated 1912 on Elo and 9 in the world, Mexico arrived as a genuine contender. Javier Aguirre's side topped their group, winning 4, drawing 0 and losing 0 of 4 games while scoring 8 and conceding 0. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

Their identity is control and containment, low-event games decided by the odd moment they are set up to provide. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.20 expected goals for and 0.54 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they win 2-0 against South Africa, and the expected-goals split (1.5 to 0.1) says they made the better chances. A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for. In their second group game they win 1-0 against South Korea, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (0.5 to 0.9 on xG). The scoreline was kinder than the performance, and they will know it. In their third group game they win 3-0 against Czechia, and the expected-goals split (1.8 to 0.5) says they made the better chances. The margin flattered no one; they were that far ahead. In the round-of-32 tie they win 2-0 against Ecuador, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.0 to 0.7 on xG). A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for.

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 8 goals from roughly 4.8 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Czechia, worth 1.8 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Raúl Jiménez (44), Orbelín Pineda (12), Edson Álvarez (7), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 0 and allowing only about 2.2 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from South Korea, who worked 0.9 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 4 clean sheets from 4 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Julián Quiñones (2) heading a list that also features Álvaro Fidalgo (1), Raúl Jiménez (1), Mateo Chávez (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of +8 from 9 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

They are into the knockouts now, where the 1912 rating and the xG edge matter less than holding a lead for ninety tense minutes. On this evidence they have the tools; whether they have the nerve is the one thing no model prices.
