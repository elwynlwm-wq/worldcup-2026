Netherlands came into the 2026 World Cup as a genuine contender, seeded 7 in the world and rated 1980 on Elo. Ronald Koeman's side topped their group, winning 2, drawing 2 and losing 0 of 4 games while scoring 11 and conceding 5. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

This is a front-foot side that both makes chances and denies them, the profile of a team built to go deep. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.75 expected goals for and 0.74 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they drew 2-2 against Japan, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (0.8 to 0.6 on xG). An even game that an even result did justice to. In their second group game they won 5-1 against Sweden, and they carried the greater threat, 2.6 to 1.0 on xG. A statement result that left little room for argument. In their third group game they won 3-1 against Tunisia, and the openings fell their way, 1.9 expected goals to 0.6. Not spectacular, but exactly the sort of result good sides grind out. In the round-of-32 tie they drew 1-1 with Morocco before penalties settled it. There was nothing to separate the sides in normal time, and the shootout is where models hand over to nerve.

## Attack

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 11 goals from roughly 5.2 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Sweden, worth 2.6 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Memphis Depay (55), Cody Gakpo (19), Wout Weghorst (14), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 5 and allowing only about 2.2 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Sweden, who worked 1.0 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 4 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Brian Brobbey (3) heading a list that also features Crysencio Summerville (2), Cody Gakpo (2), Virgil van Dijk (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

## The key men

Individually, Crysencio Summerville has been the driver, 2 goals and 1 assist in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.7. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of +6 from 7 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 14th for expected goals created and 2nd for goals scored, while the defence ranks 10th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

## What's next

They are into the knockouts, where the 1980 rating and the xG edge matter less than holding a lead for ninety tense minutes.
