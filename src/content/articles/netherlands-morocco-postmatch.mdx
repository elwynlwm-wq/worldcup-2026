## How it unfolded

The model favoured the Netherlands. The market favoured the Netherlands. Morocco, as they have made a habit of doing, did not much care.

For 72 minutes this followed the script. The Dutch controlled possession, Cody Gakpo's opener looked like the goal that would settle it, and Ronald Koeman's side seemed to be managing the tie towards a comfortable exit lane. Then the thing that keeps happening to good teams against Morocco happened again. The equaliser arrived in the first minute of stoppage time, Issa Diop rising when it mattered, and a game the Netherlands thought they had won turned into the game Morocco always wanted: a shootout.

From twelve yards there is only one favourite when Yassine Bounou is in the other goal. Morocco held their nerve, Bounou did the rest, and the Netherlands were left staring at their earliest World Cup exit in memory. If it felt familiar, it should. This is the second consecutive World Cup in which Morocco have eliminated the Dutch, and the echoes of their 2022 semi-final run are getting louder.

The tactical read is uncomfortable for every remaining favourite. Morocco under Mohamed Ouahbi defend deep and with total discipline, they carry a genuine threat on set pieces, and they are ice-cold in the exact scenario, a level game grinding towards penalties, that undoes technically superior sides. You can dominate the ball against them for 80 minutes and still lose, because they have built a team designed to make possession meaningless and moments decisive.

Next comes Canada, the co-hosts, in a Round of 16 tie that now looks very different. We already made Morocco favourites for that one, and this performance only hardens the case: Canada's fairytale meets the tournament's most ruthless closers. The Dutch, meanwhile, go home to a familiar post-mortem about a golden generation that promised more than it delivered.

## What it means

Some teams win tournaments by being the best. Morocco keep advancing by being the hardest to beat, and on this evidence, nobody left in the draw fancies drawing them.
