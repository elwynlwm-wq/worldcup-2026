Rated 1918 on Elo and 23 in the world, Norway arrived as a seeded side with real quality. Stale Solbakken's side advanced as group runners-up, winning 4, drawing 0 and losing 1 of 5 games while scoring 12 and conceding 9. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

They are an attacking team first, happy to trade chances and back their forwards to win the exchange. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.83 expected goals for and 1.42 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## Attack

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 12 goals from roughly 9.2 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Iraq, worth 2.5 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Erling Haaland (55), Alexander Sorloth (26), Antonio Nusa (8), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 9 from around 7.1 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Brazil, who worked 1.9 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 5 games.

In this tournament the scoring has leaned on Erling Haaland, whose 4 make up over half of the group-stage goals. That is production and dependency in one number: mark him out and the supply can dry up.

## The key men

Individually, Erling Haaland has been the driver, 5 goals in 4 appearances at an average match rating of 8.07. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers back the results: they created meaningfully more than they allowed, and that gap is usually the difference between a good run and an early flight home. A goal difference of +3 from 6 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 8th for expected goals created and 4th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 26th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.
