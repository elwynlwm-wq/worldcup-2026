Paraguay came into the 2026 World Cup as a dangerous outsider, seeded 37 in the world and rated 1815 on Elo. Gustavo Alfaro's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 2 and losing 1 of 4 games while scoring 3 and conceding 5. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

They sit in the middle of the spectrum, neither dominant nor fragile, a side that has to earn everything it gets. Across the games tracked they averaged 0.37 expected goals for and 1.39 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they lost 1-4 against United States, and the expected goals (0.5 to 1.4) had them second best on the run of play. No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In their second group game they won 1-0 against Turkiye, and they were out-created, 0.3 to 2.2 on xG. The scoreline was kinder than the performance, and they will know it. In their third group game they drew 0-0 against Australia, and it stayed tight on xG, 0.2 to 0.6. An even game that an even result did justice to. In the round-of-32 tie they drew 1-1 with Germany before penalties settled it. There was nothing to separate the sides in normal time, and the shootout is where models hand over to nerve.

## Attack

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 3 goals from roughly 1.1 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against United States, worth 0.5 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Miguel Almirón (9), Antonio Sanabria (7), Kaku (5), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 5 from around 4.2 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Turkiye, who worked 2.2 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 2 clean sheets from 4 games.

In this tournament the scoring has leaned on Mauricio, whose 1 make up over half of the group-stage goals. That is production and dependency in one number: mark him out and the supply can dry up.

## The key men

Individually, Julio Enciso has been the driver, 2 assists in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 6.93. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

The team news matters too. Availability doubts hang over D. Gómez (a booking away from a ban), M. Almirón (suspended), and how many of those clear up before kickoff shapes what this side can actually put on the pitch.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers carry a warning: they were out-chanced more often than the table shows, and results built on that footing tend not to hold up against the best. A goal difference of -2 from 4 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 47th for expected goals created and 28th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 30th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

## What's next

They are into the knockouts, where the 1815 rating and the xG edge matter less than holding a lead for ninety tense minutes.
