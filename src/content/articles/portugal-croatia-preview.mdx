Two men who have carried their countries for two decades, both the wrong side of 40, both out of second chances. If you like your football with a lump in the throat, this is your tie.

Portugal are the deeper side by a distance. Cristiano Ronaldo, absurdly, is still scoring at 41, and around him Roberto Martínez has a midfield most nations would envy: Bruno Fernandes pulling strings, Vitinha and João Neves doing the running, Rafael Leão and Pedro Neto to break lines. On paper it is a mismatch.

Croatia don't read paper. Luka Modrić, at 40, still slows a game down to his own pulse, and Mateo Kovačić beside him means Portugal will have to earn every touch in the middle. Zlatko Dalić's teams don't beat themselves, and in a knockout they are the last side you want across the halfway line when it's tight.

## By the numbers

The supporting numbers lean the same way. Portugal have conceded once in three games, Croatia have shipped as many as they have scored, and across ten meetings Portugal have won seven to Croatia's one, the most recent a 1-1 draw in 2024. This is less a gap in class than a gap in control, and in a knockout that usually favours the side that concedes least.

## The call

The model makes Portugal favourites at 57%, and the market agrees at a shorter 53%, which tells you plenty: everyone fancies Portugal, nobody's confident. Expect Portugal to go through. Don't expect Modrić to make it comfortable.
