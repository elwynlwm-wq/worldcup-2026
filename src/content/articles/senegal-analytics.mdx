On the FIFA ranking Senegal sit 18, and the Elo model has them at 1842, which frames them as a seeded side with real quality. Pape Thiaw's side were eliminated in the group stage, winning 1, drawing 0 and losing 3 of 4 games while scoring 10 and conceding 9. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

They are an attacking team first, happy to trade chances and back their forwards to win the exchange. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.78 expected goals for and 1.39 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they lost 1-3 against France, and the expected goals (0.5 to 1.8) had them second best on the run of play. No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In their second group game they lost 2-3 against Norway, and neither side made much, 1.7 to 2.2 on the underlying numbers. A defeat that leaves questions without offering many answers. In their third group game they won 5-0 against Iraq, and the openings fell their way, 3.1 expected goals to 0.2. The margin flattered no one; they were that far ahead. In the round-of-32 tie they lost 2-3 to Belgium after extra time. The better side went through, and the underlying numbers do not argue otherwise.

## Attack

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 10 goals from roughly 5.3 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Iraq, worth 3.1 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Sadio Mane (55), Ismaila Sarr (19), Nicolas Jackson (8), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 9 from around 4.2 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Norway, who worked 2.2 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 1 clean sheet from 4 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Ismaïla Sarr (3) heading a list that also features Pape Gueye (2), Iliman Ndiaye (1), Ibrahim Mbaye (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

## The key men

Individually, Ismaïla Sarr has been the driver, 3 goals and 1 assist in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.4. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

The team news matters too. Availability doubts hang over É. Mendy (twisted knee), and how many of those clear up before kickoff shapes what this side can actually put on the pitch.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms. A goal difference of +1 from 0 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 12th for expected goals created and 7th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 31st for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

## The reckoning

Their tournament is over. It ended in the round of 32, a 2-3 defeat to Belgium on 1 July. The margins told the story, and the better side went through. The profile above is the record they leave behind, and the underlying numbers say they were closer to the level than the exit alone suggests.
