South Korea came into the 2026 World Cup as a dangerous outsider, seeded 31 in the world and rated 1723 on Elo. Hong Myung-bo's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 0 and losing 2 of 3 games while scoring 2 and conceding 3. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

Their identity is control and containment, low-event games decided by the odd moment they are set up to provide. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.40 expected goals for and 0.82 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they win 2-1 against Czechia, and the expected-goals split (2.3 to 0.8) says they made the better chances. A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for. In their second group game they defeat 0-1 against Mexico, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (0.9 to 0.5 on xG). They deserved more than nothing, which will sting all the more for it. In their third group game they defeat 0-1 against South Africa, and the chances were shared fairly evenly (1.0 to 1.1 on xG). A defeat that leaves questions without offering many answers.

In attack the chances outran the returns: about 4.2 expected goals for only 2 scored. The creation is there, and if the finishing catches up they are more dangerous than the goal tally suggests. Their most threatening display came against Czechia, worth 2.3 xG on its own. Defensively they were miserly, conceding 3 and allowing only about 2.5 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from South Africa, who worked 1.1 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 3 games.

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms.
