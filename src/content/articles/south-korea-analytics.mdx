South Korea came into the 2026 World Cup as a dangerous outsider, seeded 31 in the world and rated 1723 on Elo. Hong Myung-bo's side reached the knockout rounds, winning 1, drawing 0 and losing 2 of 3 games while scoring 2 and conceding 3. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

Their identity is control and containment, low-event games decided by the odd moment they are set up to provide. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.40 expected goals for and 0.82 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they won 2-1 against Czechia, and the expected-goals split (2.3 to 0.8) says they made the better chances. A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for. In their second group game they lost 0-1 against Mexico, and neither side made much, 0.9 to 0.5 on the underlying numbers. They deserved more than nothing, which will sting all the more for it. In their third group game they lost 0-1 against South Africa, and it stayed tight on xG, 1.0 to 1.1. A defeat that leaves questions without offering many answers.

## Attack

In attack the chances outran the returns: about 4.2 expected goals for only 2 scored. The creation is there, and if the finishing catches up they are more dangerous than the goal tally suggests. Their most threatening display came against Czechia, worth 2.3 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Son Heung-min (56), Hwang Hee-chan (17), Lee Jae-sung (15), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were miserly, conceding 3 and allowing only about 2.5 expected goals in the tracked games. A back line that gives up so little turns knockout football into a coin toss it can win from a single set piece. The heaviest pressure they faced came from South Africa, who worked 1.1 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 0 clean sheets from 3 games.

In this tournament the scoring has leaned on Oh Hyeon-Gyu, whose 1 make up over half of the group-stage goals. That is production and dependency in one number: mark him out and the supply can dry up.

## The key men

Individually, Hwang In-beom has been the driver, 1 goal and 1 assist in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.47. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms. A goal difference of -1 from 3 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 19th for expected goals created and 33rd for goals scored, while the defence ranks 14th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

## What's next

They are into the knockouts, where the 1723 rating and the xG edge matter less than holding a lead for ninety tense minutes.
