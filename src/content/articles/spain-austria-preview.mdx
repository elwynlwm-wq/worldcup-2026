This is the tactical tie of the round: the best passing side left against the best pressing side left.

Spain are a joy when they get going. Lamine Yamal, still only 18, plays like he owns the place, Pedro González López pulls the strings and Rodri sits behind it all making the whole thing tick. When they string it together nobody in the tournament keeps the ball better.

Austria's plan is to never let them string it together. Ralf Rangnick has built a side in his own image: relentless, aggressive, hunting the ball in packs the second it's lost. David Alaba organises the back, Marcel Sabitzer and Konrad Laimer cover every blade of grass, and Marko Arnautović is still a nuisance up top. Suffocate Spain, turn it into a scrap, and anything can happen.

## By the numbers

Spain arrive without having conceded a goal: five scored, none against, and an expected-goals line of 1.75 created to 0.18 allowed that is the meanest in the tournament. Austria have been leakier, giving up more xG than they make, and beyond Marko Arnautović they lack a repeatable way to hurt a back line this organised. History matches the mood, Spain winning nine of sixteen meetings and running out 5-1 the last time the two met. Austria need it scrappy and set-piece-led; Spain will want it exactly as controlled as their group stage.

## The call

The model makes Spain heavy favourites at 82%, the market a touch warier at 73%. That gap is the whole match: if Spain get room, it's comfortable; if Austria get in their faces, it's a long night. Back Spain, but keep the remote nearby.
