Switzerland are the clear favourites here, and the underlying numbers make the case even more strongly than the group table does.

Murat Yakin's side won Group B with seven points and, tellingly, created chances at a rate their opponents could not live with — a 3.2 expected-goals night in a 1-1 draw with Qatar they should have won at a canter, a 4-1 dismantling of Bosnia, and a 2-1 win over Canada. In Breel Embolo up top and the metronomic Granit Xhaka and Remo Freuler in midfield, they have a settled, experienced spine. The only concern is a minor one: Miro Muheim is nursing a calf strain, a rotation question rather than a crisis.

Algeria arrive with talent but doubts. Vladimir Petković's side scraped through, a heavy 3-0 loss to Argentina bookended by a 2-1 win over Jordan and a wild 3-3 draw with Austria, and their form has been streaky. Riyad Mahrez, still their talisman with 38 international goals, can win any game on his own, but the bigger story is a fitness one: Mohamed Amoura, their in-form 19-goal forward, is battling a hamstring injury, and Algeria are a markedly less dangerous team without his running.

History offers Algeria little comfort either — Switzerland have won both previous meetings without conceding, albeit the last was back in 1986. The tactical shape figures to be Switzerland controlling possession and territory, with Algeria hoping Mahrez can conjure something on the counter. If Amoura cannot go, that plan loses much of its threat.

We expect Switzerland to advance. They are the better-organised side, they dominate the chance creation, and Algeria's biggest attacking weapon may be watching from the bench. Algeria's hope is a Mahrez moment and a low-scoring scrap; Switzerland's job is to avoid exactly that.

Prediction: Switzerland to win, probably comfortably if Amoura misses out, though a Mahrez spark could keep Algeria in it.
