On paper this one meant nothing. The United States had already secured top spot in Group D, Türkiye were already out, and both managers picked accordingly. On the pitch it was one of the games of the group stage, settled in the eighth minute of stoppage time.

It started at a sprint. Auston Trusty headed the USA in front inside three minutes, and for a moment it looked like a routine sign-off for a side with nothing to play for. Türkiye had other ideas. Arda Güler, the tournament's most watchable teenager not named Yamal, levelled almost immediately, and Barış Alper Yılmaz turned the game on its head before the half hour. Sebastian Berhalter's second-half strike hauled the USA back to 2-2, and a dead rubber had somehow become a proper contest.

The finish was pure theatre. Deep into stoppage time, with a draw looking the fair result, Kaan Ayhan popped up in the 98th minute to win it, sparking scenes more befitting a knockout tie than a game with nothing on it. For Türkiye, eliminated and heading home, it was the sweetest kind of consolation: a farewell with a flourish, and a reminder of the talent, Güler above all, they will build around next time.

For the USA, the defeat was, as their manager admitted, meaningless in the only sense that matters. They finished top, rested legs where they could, and will feel the group-stage box is comfortably ticked. But there is a note of caution in a defence that shipped three, even a second-string one, and in the ease with which Türkiye's forwards found joy. Top spot buys you a kinder draw; it does not paper over the odd soft goal.

Mostly, though, this was a night to enjoy for its own sake. Two teams with the handbrake off, five goals, a last-kick winner, and Arda Güler announcing himself to anyone still not paying attention. Not every classic needs stakes. Some just need players willing to go for it, and a 98th-minute finish to send everyone home talking.
