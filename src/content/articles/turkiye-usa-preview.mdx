This is the hardest kind of game to call, because it is the kind that means almost nothing on paper. The United States have already secured top spot in Group D, Türkiye are already eliminated, and both managers will treat the team sheet accordingly. When the stakes vanish, so does the usefulness of the form guide.

Our model still nudges towards Türkiye, who are marginally the higher-rated side, but rotation flattens everything. Expect the USA to rest key legs ahead of the knockouts, and expect Türkiye, with nothing left to lose, to throw caution aside and give their supporters a proper send-off. That combination, a second-string favourite against a liberated underdog, is a recipe for an open, unpredictable game rather than a controlled one.

The one thing worth watching is the young talent. Türkiye's Arda Güler is among the most exciting teenagers in the tournament, and a low-pressure setting is exactly where a player like that can express himself. For the USA, it is a chance to give squad players minutes and fringe attackers a run.

Predicting a dead rubber is a mug's game, so we will not pretend to be confident. If pushed: lean Türkiye on pure motivation, but expect goals at both ends and a scoreline nobody would have forecast if the stakes had been real.

Türkiye edge a tight head-to-head, three wins to two in six meetings, and carry real threat in Hakan Çalhanoğlu. The United States may be without a fully fit Christian Pulišić, nursing a calf strain. Two sides with similar expected-goals profiles, which is usually where small margins decide it.

Prediction: a loose, entertaining draw or a narrow Türkiye win, with the handbrake off and the result close to a coin-flip.
