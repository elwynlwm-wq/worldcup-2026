On the FIFA ranking United States sit 15, and the Elo model has them at 1781, which frames them as a genuine contender. Mauricio Pochettino's side topped their group, winning 3, drawing 0 and losing 2 of 5 games while scoring 11 and conceding 8. What follows is the tournament read off the data rather than the highlights reel.

## How they play

They sit in the middle of the spectrum, neither dominant nor fragile, a side that has to earn everything it gets. Across the games tracked they averaged 1.21 expected goals for and 1.28 against, and the shape of that number, more than the results, is the honest measure of them.

## The campaign, game by game

Game by game, the campaign told its own story. In their opening group game they won 4-1 against Paraguay, and the expected-goals split (1.4 to 0.5) says they made the better chances. It was the kind of afternoon where everything they tried came off. In their second group game they won 2-0 against Australia, and they carried the greater threat, 1.1 to 0.3 on xG. Control when it mattered, and enough at the other end to see it through. In their third group game they lost 2-3 against Turkiye, and the better chances went the other way, 2.0 to 3.2 on expected goals. No arguments about the result; they were beaten by the better side on the day. In the round-of-32 tie they beat Bosnia and Herzegovina 2-0, and the expected-goals split (0.9 to 0.2) says they made the better chances. A win earned rather than gifted, and taken with the composure the occasion asked for. In the round-of-16 tie they lost 1-4 to Belgium, and the run of chances favoured Belgium, 0.6 to 2.1 on xG. The better side went through, and the underlying numbers do not argue otherwise.

## Attack

In attack the finishing ran ahead of the process: 11 goals from roughly 6.1 expected. That is a fine line to walk, because clinical runs tend to regress, and a side relying on beating its xG is a side living slightly on credit. Their most threatening display came against Turkiye, worth 2.0 xG on its own. The pedigree up front sits with Christian Pulisic (33), Ricardo Pepi (13), Weston McKennie (12), career international goals in brackets, the names that set the ceiling.

## Defence

Defensively they were steady without being watertight, conceding 8 from around 6.4 expected. Solid enough to build on, not so solid that they can switch off. The heaviest pressure they faced came from Turkiye, who worked 3.2 xG against them, the game to learn most from. In total they kept 2 clean sheets from 5 games.

The group-stage goals were spread around, Folarin Balogun (2) heading a list that also features Sebastian Berhalter (1), Giovanni Reyna (1), Damian Bobadilla (1). A side scoring from several places is a harder side to plan against.

## The key men

Individually, Folarin Balogun has been the driver, 2 goals in 3 appearances at an average match rating of 7.75. Sides that lean on one player peak when he does and dip when he is quiet, and that dependence is worth watching in a knockout.

The team news matters too. Availability doubts hang over C. Roldan (muscle bruise), C. Pulišić (calf muscle strain), and how many of those clear up before kickoff shapes what this side can actually put on the pitch.

## What the numbers say

Pulling it together, the underlying numbers sit close to even, the mark of a side that has to fight for every result rather than one that overwhelms. A goal difference of +3 from 6 group points is the surface record; the match-by-match expected-goals picture in the panel above is the version that travels.

Set against the other 47 teams in the field, their attack ranks 15th for expected goals created and 7th for goals scored, while the defence ranks 29th for expected goals allowed. That is the truer placing, stripped of who they happened to draw, and it is the number to hold onto when the bracket starts throwing up mismatches and upsets.

## The reckoning

Their tournament is over. It ended in the last 16, a 1-4 defeat to Belgium on 7 July. The margins told the story, and the better side went through. The profile above is the record they leave behind, and the underlying numbers say they were closer to the level than the exit alone suggests.
